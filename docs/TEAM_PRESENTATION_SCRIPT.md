## 🔵 PART 3: THE INTELLIGENCE & TECH (Slides 7 - 14)

#### Slide 7: Behind the Scenes (Tech Stack)

#### Slide 8: The Data Clues (Features)

"To train our AI, we used 4 different datasets. Instead of just looking at the IP address, our AI looks at **41 different clues**—like how long a connection lasted or how many bytes were sent."

#### Slide 9: Our AI Choice (Random Forest)

"For our 'Brain,' we chose the **Random Forest** method. Think of it as a committee of 100 mini-brains that vote together. This makes the predictions very stable and hard for hackers to trick."

#### Slide 10: AI Competition (Benchmarking)

"We tested many methods, including SVM and KNN. In our tests, the 'Random Forest' committee was the clear winner, catching almost every single attack we threw at it."

#### Slide 11: The Explainer (SHAP Logic)

"This is our most important feature. We used something called **SHAP** to 'open up' the AI brain. It tells us exactly which clue made the AI suspicious. No more guessing!"

#### Slide 12: Real-World Testing

"We didn't just test it once. we ran simulations across different network types—like home setups and office setups—to make sure the AI is ready for the real world."
